YearHome valueEquivalent rentPaid inEquityNet if soldInvestment gainHouse vs. market

Paid in is the down payment, closing costs, and every mortgage, tax, insurance, HOA and other-cost payment, less any rent revenue collected. Net if sold = equity − selling costs + rent you didn't pay − paid in. Investment gain is what the down payment plus closing costs would have earned in the market instead — the growth only, not the principal, so it's directly comparable to Net if sold. It reads green in years the market is ahead of the house and red once the house has passed it. House vs. market is Net if sold minus Investment gain — positive means buying came out ahead of putting the same cash in the market. Other costs covers maintenance, vacancy and turnover on a rental, or interior repairs and a reserve against special assessments on a condo. Taxes, insurance, HOA and other costs rise with escrow inflation; rent revenue rises with rent inflation. Excludes maintenance, vacancy, income tax on rent, and mortgage interest deduction.
